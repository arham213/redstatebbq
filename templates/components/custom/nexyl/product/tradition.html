The Alabama
Tradition

Alabama white sauce is the South's odd one out mayonnaise and vinegar instead of tomato, born in north Alabama for smoked chicken. We kept the tang and the creaminess but built ours to stand up to beef, leaning on horseradish so it can hold its own against a smoked prime rib.

The Horseradish is the
Whole Point

Most Alabama white sauces are built for chicken. We wanted one that could stand up to beef so we leaned on the horseradish. It's sharp, a little showy, and exactly why this works on a smoked prime rib where a sweet sauce would just disappear.
